Ethical Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2025

Premier Ability Care is a UK-based care organisation dedicated to delivering compassionate, person-centred support for individuals of all ages and abilities, including elderly residents and individuals with complex physical or mental health needs. Our mission is to create safe, supportive, and respectful environments where every person is treated with dignity, kindness, and the care they truly deserve.

Background and Rationale

With a growing ageing population in the UK and an increasing prevalence of dementia among those aged 65 and over, there is a significant need for high-quality, ethical, and personalised dementia care services. Individuals living with dementia often face memory loss, confusion, and reduced independence, which creates unique safeguarding and wellbeing concerns.

Premier Ability Care aims to meet these needs through regulated, flexible services delivered by trained professionals, focused on preserving independence and enhancing quality of life.

Aim and Objectives

The primary aim of this service is to provide high-quality, ethical, person-centred care for individuals aged 65 and over who have been diagnosed with dementia or Alzheimer’s disease.

  • To deliver personalised care plans that respect life history, preferences, and cultural values
  • To promote and maintain independence for as long as possible
  • To enhance quality of life through engagement, companionship, and emotional support
  • To ensure safety and physical wellbeing through tailored support
  • To support family carers through respite and professional guidance

Target Participant Group

  • Individuals aged 65 and over
  • Diagnosed with dementia or Alzheimer’s disease
  • Living in community, supported living, or requiring live-in care
  • May have additional physical, sensory, or mental health needs

Description of Services Provided

  • Personal Care & Daily Living Support: Respectful assistance with hygiene, dressing, eating, and other daily needs
  • Medication Assistance: Safe support with reminders and administration under care plans
  • Companionship & Social Engagement: Stimulating activities, conversation, and emotional support
  • Mobility & Safety Support: Safe movement, fall prevention, and aid usage
  • Specialist Dementia Care: Person-centred techniques such as:
    • Compassionate communication
    • Routines tailored to habits
    • Behavioural support
    • Life story work
  • End-of-Life (Palliative) Care: Comfort-focused support for individuals and families
  • Additional Services: Live-in care, respite care, and supported living arrangements

Ethical Principles and Safeguards

  • Respect for Autonomy and Dignity: Choice-led care; best interest decisions where capacity is lacking
  • Confidentiality and Data Protection: GDPR-compliant, secure record-keeping, limited access to care professionals only
  • Safety and Safeguarding: Trained staff, safeguarding policies, and responsive procedures
  • Person-Centred Care: Individually developed plans, compassionate staff approach, regular reviews
  • Equality and Non-Discrimination: Inclusive care respectful of cultural, religious, and individual differences
  • Minimisation of Harm: Calm handling of distress, use of dementia-friendly environments

Consent Process

We obtain informed consent where individuals have capacity. For those lacking capacity, we follow the Mental Capacity Act 2005, involving families or advocates as appropriate. Information is always provided in accessible formats to aid understanding.

Staff Qualifications and Training

All staff are trained and regularly assessed in:

  • Dementia awareness and communication
  • Managing behaviours that challenge
  • Safeguarding and adult protection
  • Medication handling
  • Infection control
  • Person-centred care delivery

Risk Management

Potential risks include emotional distress, falls, and confusion. We manage these through:

  • Tailored risk assessments
  • Familiar care staff assignments
  • De-escalation and communication techniques
  • Environmental safety modifications

Quality Assurance

We are fully regulated by the Care Quality Commission (CQC). Quality is maintained through:

  • Ongoing audits of care delivery
  • Feedback systems for clients and families
  • Continuous improvement processes

Organisational Values

  • Dignity First: Respecting privacy, choices, and individuality
  • Compassion Always: Delivering care with kindness and patience
  • Tailored Support: Recognising and planning for unique personal needs

This Ethical Policy is designed to uphold the highest standards of dignity, professionalism, and respect for human rights—supporting individuals with dementia to lead fulfilling and meaningful lives.
